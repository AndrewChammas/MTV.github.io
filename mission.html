Mission profile and scientific objectives

Mission profile

Mission to Venus will launch at Cape Canaveral, Florida in 2035. The payload will be launched by "Falcon Heavy" and taken into a low Earth orbit where the payload will be depolyed. The payload will remain in a orbit around the Earth untill all systems are verified to be functional. Then "The RIDE" (kickbooster) will fire it's engine and propell the payload to Venus. Once the burn is complete, "The RIDE" will detatch from the rest of the payload, allowing the payload to transfer to Venus. Once detached, "The RIDE" will do a small burn to change it's trajectory, aiming to the surface of Venus. This stage will burn during atmospheric entry and therefore ensure that there is no space debris. Once the payload has reached Venus, the "CHONCC" will preform a capture burn. After the capture burn is complete, the payload stays in orbit untill a suitable landing site is determined. Once the site is found, and aeroshell containing both "PATRICC" (Lander) and "VAPOR" (Blimp) will detach from "CHONCC" and start an entry burn. After atmospheric entry both "PATRICC" and "VAPOR", will seperate and depoly. "VAPOR" will inflate in atmosphere while "PATRICC" will depoly a parachute. Once both are fully depolyed, anylais and research may begin.

Scientific Objectives

Once "CHONCC", "VAPOR" and "PATRICC" are in their respective depolyment zones. Scientific investigations can take place. The three main objectives are outline below:

  • Understand the history of volatiles and liquid water on Venus and history of habitability
  • Understand coupling between surface and the atmosphere
  • Understand historic geological activity and if Venus is active today

Each will carry a range of scientific instruments. "PATRICC" will determine mineral compositions as well as taking surface images. "VAPOR" will act as a floating "scientific lab", taking atmospheric readings as well as ariel pictures. "CHONCC" will take high resolution images of Venus as well as relaying all data back to earth.
